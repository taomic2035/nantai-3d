# Infinite chunk boundary continuity audit

Date: 2026-07-21
Scope: `pipeline.mock_layout.MockLayoutGenerator`

## Result

The procedural layout now uses world-boundary identities for every feature
that crosses a chunk edge:

- the east endpoint of an east-west main road is byte-equal to the west
  endpoint generated by the adjacent chunk;
- both north-south trail exits are byte-equal to the corresponding entries
  generated by the adjacent chunk;
- stream presence is selected per world row rather than per chunk, and stream
  endpoint coordinates are shared across east-west neighbors.

This is a **C0 position-continuity contract**. It prevents roads, trails, and
streams from jumping or disappearing when an on-demand chunk loads. It does
not claim tangent continuity, terrain-height continuity, physically simulated
hydrology, or measured real-world geometry.

## Prior failure

The previous implementation claimed that adjacent chunk roads were aligned,
but independently derived each chunk's boundary values:

```text
main road east/west seam delta: +2 m or -8 m
trail count and x anchors: independently random per chunk
stream presence: selected from both chunk x and y
```

Consequently, an arbitrary-coordinate traversal could expose:

- a visible lateral jump in the main road;
- a trail ending at a chunk border with no continuation;
- a stream appearing or disappearing exactly at a streamed boundary.

The defect was present in the layout source, not in Viewer scheduling or PLY
streaming.

## Corrected identity contract

Every crossing point is now derived from:

```text
SHA-256(world_seed, feature namespace, world boundary x, world boundary y, lane)
```

The generator uses the same world grid-line identity on both sides of a
boundary. It does not use Python's process-randomized `hash()` and does not
infer continuity from neighboring files. Negative coordinates and different
world seeds use the same canonical calculation.

Two separated trail lanes are always generated. Their interior point is
calculated on the local main-road centerline, so the local trail-to-road
connection is explicit rather than visual-only.

Stream rows are deterministic from `(world_seed, chunk_y)`. Within a selected
row, each chunk shares its west/east endpoint with its neighbors. This avoids
an unproven stream terminus at a chunk edge while retaining deterministic local
bends.

## Remaining boundary work

This correction makes route and stream topology safe to consume across
on-demand chunk transitions. Further realism still requires separately
verified contracts for:

1. terrain elevation and surface-normal seams;
2. road/stream mesh width and material UV continuity;
3. vegetation and building exclusion zones across chunk edges;
4. C1 or spline tangent continuity where a renderer exposes sharp bends;
5. authored transition modules for road, terrace, forest, stream, and
   settlement changes.

The next image2 design pack targets item 5. Those images remain replaceable
design inputs and cannot prove geometry, calibration, or multiview coverage.
